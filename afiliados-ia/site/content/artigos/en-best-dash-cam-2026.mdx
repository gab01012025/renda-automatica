## Buying Guide

### What to Look For

1. **Resolution:** Higher resolutions like 4K offer clearer images but may take up more storage space.
   
2. **Channels:** Dual-channel dash cams record both the front and the back of the car, which can provide more comprehensive coverage.

3. **Night Vision:** Essential for driving in low-light conditions, ensuring clear footage regardless of the time of day.

4. **Storage Options:** Consider models with expandable storage options or those that offer cloud storage.

5. **Ease of Use:** Look for a user-friendly interface and easy installation process, especially if you're not tech-savvy.

6. **Durability:** For those in extreme climates, models with heat and water resistance could be worth the investment.

7. **Connectivity:** Some models offer app connectivity for remote access to footage, which can be convenient for frequent travelers.

## FAQ

### 1. Do all dash cams have built-in GPS?

No, not all models come with built-in GPS. Some require additional purchases or rely on smartphone connectivity for GPS functions.

### 2. How much storage do I need?

It depends on the resolution and length of your recordings. A 64GB card is usually a good starting point for regular use, but higher resolutions will need more.

### 3. Can dash cams drain my car battery?

Most modern dash cams are designed to minimize power consumption, but it's still a good idea to turn them off when not needed. Some models offer battery pack options.

### 4. Are dash cams legal in all countries?

While generally legal, some countries have specific privacy laws regarding recording, so always check local regulations before installing a dash cam.

### 5. Can I install a dash cam myself?

Yes, most dash cams come with simple installation instructions. However, dual-camera systems may require professional installation for optimal setup.
